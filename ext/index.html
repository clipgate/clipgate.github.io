Secret Protection

Your API keys deserve better
than plaintext storage.

Most browser clipboard tools store everything in plain text. ClipGate encrypts secrets, masks them in the UI, and auto-deletes them. Because a clipboard extension that leaks your credentials is worse than no extension at all.

18 Secret Patterns

AWS, GitHub, GitLab, Slack, Stripe, OpenAI, Anthropic, npm, PyPI, SendGrid, Twilio, Heroku, private keys, generic credentials, and high-entropy strings via Shannon analysis.

AES-256-GCM at Rest

Every detected secret is encrypted before touching IndexedDB. The key is stored separately in chrome.storage. Not visible in DevTools, not readable in disk forensics.

Configurable Auto-Expiry

Default: 2 hours. Max: 2 days. Secrets are purged every 60 seconds and filtered from the UI even before purge runs. Your call, your timeline.

Smart Masking

Shows the recognizable prefix (sk_live_, AKIA, ghp_) plus masked middle plus last 4 characters. You know which key it is without seeing the full secret.

Questions?

Common questions about ClipGate

Does it work offline?

Yes. ClipGate works 100% offline. Everything happens in your browser. No internet connection needed, no data sent anywhere.

What if I uninstall it?

Your clips stay in your browser profile's storage. Uninstalling the extension removes it from your browser but doesn't touch stored data. Reinstall anytime to recover.

Can anyone see my clips?

No. Clips are stored locally in your browser. Secrets are AES-256 encrypted. No cloud sync, no accounts, no third parties have access.

How much storage does it use?

Typically 2-10MB depending on history size. You can clear old clips anytime. Secrets auto-expire, freeing space. The extension itself is ~50KB.
